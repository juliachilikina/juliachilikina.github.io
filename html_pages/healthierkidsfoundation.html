Healthier Kids

foundation

Healthier Kids Foundation is a family forward health agency that gives children and those who love them the education and cutting edge tools they rightfully deserve to live a healthy life. The organization’s website needs to be redesigned to better connect with its audience. The purpose of this redesign project is to simplyfy user experience, build more intuitive information architecture, develope visual consistency, and as a result make it easier for the users to reach their goals on the site.

Type

UX/UI, branding

Time

3 weeks

My role

UX/UI designer

Overview

Healthier Kids Foundation (HKF) is a non-profit health agency based in San Jose, CA, that provides children, their parents and care takers medical services and screenings as well as educational programs.

According to HKF's saleforce data there's thousands of mothers and children who received help with the organization and over 3,400 children who were successfully enrolled in subsidized medical insurance plans since 2016. ​ However, the inconsistent design and poorly structured content of their website are resulting in unfavorable user experience. With the growing social need for its services and increasing competitive pressure in the nonprofit industry, HKF needs an urgent visual overhaul of their website to grow its reach and impact.

Heuristic evaluation

The Ideation phase of the project began with an in-depth Heuristic Evaluation and annotations. In addition to the key user insights from the previous phases, we also learned that: The logo does not comply with the basic Gestalt Principles of UI The color palette was complex and it did not pass the color testing completely Visual inconsistencies with photography and elements throughout the website

Usability testing

The goal of conducting in-person interviews with 8 participants helped us further empathize with users and identify their main pain points. Participants were asked to perform a set of tasks on the website and their interaction with the system was observed to gather insights.

Interviewed parents, teachers, and caregivers for the Bay Area Gender: 50/50 Ethnicity: Mix Device: Laptop and Mobile Duration: 20-30 minutes each Tasks: Finding the mission of the organization, finding the vision screening program, and donate attempt ​

Identifying user needs

We gathered all the data from interviews and usability tests and organization the information into themes. After several rounds of synthesis, we were able to create an affinity diagram synthesizing the data into key themes as shown. The key insights for the team were: Users liked the platform for inexpensive healthcare alternatives for children considering the expensive Bay Area. The majority of the users were parents from low-income households whose first language was not English. Users felt confused navigating through the website content. Users found the website bold but inconsistent overall. Users did not like that the organizational structure (Mission, personnel, etc.) was unclear and that there were a lot of third-party links.

Design system

I wanted to create a color palette that is associated with kids and provokes positive emotions. There are 2 things that were important - website should look gender neutral, and colors must be bright and modern but nod standard 'medical' pallette.

In order to make the site look more friendly and soft I chose buttons with round corners. For the same reason I used google font "cabin" as it has rounded letters. It looks playful and still has high readability.

Another challenge of visual redesign was making a new logo. Initially it wasn't planed, however during our first round of interviews respondents mentioned that they like the concept of the current logo and that made us pay more attention to it. Interviewees specifically liked the use of multiple colors and the mood it conveys, when people look at the logo it represents happy children and we definitely wanted to keep that aspect. However, I found a couple of issues with existing logo that motivated me to redesign it. First of all it is very complicated and hardly responsive. When it’s small it’s not easy to differentiate 3 people and see details. Next, it exists in 1 variant - horizontal positioning where text is on the side. That makes it even less responsive and hard to use for the vertical layouts. And the last part - it resembles a restroom sign which is hard to unsee once noticed.
So taken all that to account I made a new logo. It's simple and bright, responsive and can be used for different purposes and it has soft round lines.
